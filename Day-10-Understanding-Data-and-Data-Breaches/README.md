# Day 10 - Understanding Data and Data Breaches

## Objective
Understand the different types of data that organizations protect and learn a simple framework for thinking about data security.

---

## Lesson of the Day

Today, I learned that almost every major cybersecurity incident is ultimately about **data**.

Attackers may use different techniques, but their main goal is often to steal, misuse, or gain unauthorized access to valuable data.

Understanding what data exists and why it is valuable is an important mindset for anyone working in cybersecurity.

---

## Three Categories of Data

### 1. Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

PII is information that can identify an individual.

Examples:
- Full name
- Email address
- Phone number
- Home address
- Aadhaar or Passport number
- Date of birth

If exposed, PII can lead to identity theft, fraud, or privacy violations.

---

### 2. Credentials

Credentials are pieces of information that prove you are who you claim to be.

Examples:
- Usernames
- Passwords
- PINs
- One-Time Passwords (OTPs)
- Authentication tokens
- API keys

If attackers obtain credentials, they may be able to access accounts or systems without authorization.

---

### 3. Proprietary Data

Proprietary data belongs to an organization and provides business value.

Examples:
- Source code
- Internal documents
- Business strategies
- Customer databases
- Financial records
- Research and development data

If stolen, proprietary data can result in financial loss, reputational damage, and competitive disadvantage.

---

## The Three Questions to Ask

Today's lesson introduced a simple way to think like a security professional.

Whenever I look at a system, application, or database, I should ask:

### 1. What data does this hold?

Identify the information stored in the system.

---

### 2. Which of the three categories does it belong to?

- Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
- Credentials
- Proprietary Data

Some systems may contain more than one category.

---

### 3. Who would want it, and what would they do with it?

Think from an attacker's perspective.

Examples:
- Identity theft
- Financial fraud
- Account takeover
- Corporate espionage
- Selling stolen data

This mindset helps identify why data needs protection.

---

## What I Learned

- Cyberattacks are often focused on stealing valuable data.
- Not all data has the same purpose or value.
- Data can be classified into PII, Credentials, and Proprietary Data.
- Thinking like an attacker helps identify security risks.
- Asking the right questions is the first step toward protecting information.

---

## Key Takeaways

✅ Every major data breach involves valuable information.

✅ Data generally falls into three categories:
- Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
- Credentials
- Proprietary Data

✅ Before protecting data, understand:
1. What data exists?
2. Which category does it belong to?
3. Who would want it and why?

---

## Reflection

Today's lesson helped me realize that cybersecurity is ultimately about protecting data. Whether it is a personal account or a large organization, the goal is to understand what information is valuable and how it could be misused.

The three-question framework is simple but powerful, and I plan to use it whenever I analyze a system or application in the future.
